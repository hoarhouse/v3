What Communio is

More than a platform.
A community.

Communio is a verified global network of Catholic parishes — built to connect communities across countries, faiths, and languages so they can act together on the things that matter.

A verified, trusted network
Every parish is personally reviewed and approved by DCF before joining. That means every connection you make on Communio is real, accountable, and part of a shared mission.
Real weight at the highest levels
500 parishes co-signing a statement on ethical AI carries genuine influence at the Holy See, the EU, and the UN. Isolated, each parish is a whisper. Together, they are heard.
Built for every parish, everywhere
Whether you're in New York or rural Ghana, serving 50 or 5,000 — Communio is for you. The smallest, most isolated community deserves the same tools and the same voice as the largest.
Collaborate

Your parish doesn't
have to act alone.

Communio gives every registered parish access to a shared collaboration layer — where you can find other parishes, launch shared projects, co-sign statements, and build something together that no single parish could build alone.

Collective advocacy
Co-sign open letters, advocacy statements, and petitions that carry real institutional weight. Communio aggregates parish voices into unified positions that reach the Holy See, EU policymakers, and the UN.
Example campaign"500 parishes calling for ethical AI standards in Catholic schools — co-signed by parishes across 38 countries."
Shared projects
Launch or join collaborative projects with other parishes — joint fundraising drives, shared educational programmes, resource exchanges, and cross-border community initiatives. Find parishes by focus area, country, or language.
Example project"St. Mary's NYC and Holy Spirit Accra running a joint youth mentorship programme across two continents."
Sister parish relationships
Form direct, lasting relationships with parishes in other countries. Share resources, support each other's needs, exchange pastoral insights, and build the kind of global solidarity that gives the Church its true character.
Example connection"A parish in Warsaw forming a sister relationship with a community in rural Kenya — sharing resources and prayer intentions."

How it works

Up and running
in days, not months.

Step 01
Register in under 10 minutes
We look up your parish automatically and pre-fill what we know. Confirm the details, describe your community, and optionally connect your bank account for donations.
~10 minutes
Step 02
DCF reviews your application
Every parish is personally reviewed by the DCF team. This protects the network's integrity. You'll get an email decision with your login credentials.
3–5 business days
Step 03
Your parish goes live
Profile published on the global map. Donation page active. Dashboard access granted — build your website, post events, message other parishes, and access the collaboration layer.
Instant on approval
Step 04
Join the movement
Browse projects, launch campaigns, form sister-parish relationships, and co-sign advocacy statements. As more parishes join, the network grows stronger — and so does your voice.
