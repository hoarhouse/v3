RCIA — Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults

Thinking about
becoming Catholic?

Whether you are curious, exploring, or ready to take the next step — you are welcome here. No question is too small. No journey is too late.

What is RCIA?

A journey of faith, not a class to pass.

The Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults (RCIA) is the process through which adults enter the Catholic Church. It is a gradual journey of prayer, learning, and community that prepares you to receive the Sacraments of Initiation — Baptism, Confirmation, and the Eucharist.

At St. Mary's, our RCIA programme runs from September through Easter. Sessions are held weekly, in a welcoming, small-group setting. There is no pressure, no test, and no deadline — only an open invitation.

Who is RCIA for?

Non-Christians

Those who have never been baptised and are drawn to explore the Christian faith for the first time.

Baptised non-Catholics

Christians from other traditions who feel called to enter full communion with the Catholic Church.

Uncatechised Catholics

Baptised Catholics who never received First Communion or Confirmation and wish to complete their initiation.

Returning Catholics

Those who were away from the Church for a time and are seeking to reconnect with their faith.

The Journey

Four stages, each with its own graces and milestones.

1
Sep — Nov

Inquiry

Informal exploration. Ask any question. No commitment required. Just come and listen.

2
Nov — Feb

Catechumenate

Weekly formation in Scripture, the Creed, prayer, and Catholic life. The heart of the journey.

3
Feb — Apr

Purification

A period of deeper prayer and preparation during Lent, culminating in the Easter Vigil.

4
Easter Vigil

Initiation

Reception of the Sacraments — Baptism, Confirmation, and First Eucharist — at the Easter Vigil Mass.

Common Questions

Do I have to become Catholic at the end?
No. RCIA is a journey of discernment. Many people attend for months before deciding whether to continue. There is no pressure and no obligation.
What if I was baptised in another church?
Your Baptism is recognised by the Catholic Church. You would enter through a slightly different path called reception into full communion, rather than Baptism.
Can I bring my spouse or a friend?
Absolutely. Spouses, family members, and friends are warmly welcome to attend sessions as sponsors or companions on your journey.
When does it start and how long does it take?
Our programme begins each September and concludes at the Easter Vigil in April. Sessions are held on Sunday mornings after the 9:00 AM Mass.
Is there a cost?
There is no charge for RCIA at St. Mary's. All materials are provided. We want nothing to stand in the way of your journey.
What if I miss a session?
Life happens. Missing a session is not a problem — we will make sure you are caught up. The journey is yours to walk at your own pace.
