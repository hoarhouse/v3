Ministry

Volunteer

Share your time and talents to serve our parish and community. Whether you have an hour a week or a whole weekend, your contribution makes a real difference. Roles include hospitality, event support, parish office assistance, and more.

Available roles
Welcome Team
Sunday mornings · 1–2 hrs/week

Be the first face visitors see at St. Mary's. Greeters welcome parishioners at the church doors before Mass, distribute the weekly bulletin, help newcomers find a seat, and answer basic questions about the parish. Training and a team leader are provided.

What we are looking for
Warm, friendly, and approachable personality
Available at least one Sunday per month
No prior church experience needed
Comfortable speaking with strangers
Food Pantry Helper
Saturday mornings · 3 hrs/week

Help pack and distribute food to families in need across the Lower East Side. Volunteers sort donations, prepare food parcels, and assist clients at our weekly Saturday distribution. This is one of our most impactful community ministries.

What we are looking for
Available Saturday mornings 9 AM–12 PM
Able to lift boxes up to 25 lbs
Commitment of at least twice per month preferred
Background check required for leadership roles
Parish Office Assistant
Weekday hours · flexible

Support the parish office team with administrative tasks including answering phones, helping with data entry, preparing mailings, and welcoming visitors to the office. Great for someone who wants to be at the heart of parish life.

What we are looking for
Basic computer skills helpful
Friendly and organized
Weekday availability (flexible hours)
Discretion with confidential parish information
Event Setup & Cleanup
As needed · occasional weekends

Help set up and clean up at parish events including the annual picnic, fundraising dinners, and seasonal celebrations. A vital behind-the-scenes role that makes every parish event possible.

What we are looking for
Physical ability to move tables, chairs, and equipment
Available for occasional weekend events
Team player who doesn't mind getting hands dirty
No experience necessary
Contact: Parish Office — Maria Santos

Ministry

Small Groups

Deepen your faith in community through our small group programmes. We currently offer weekly Bible study, a men's faith group, a women's prayer circle, and seasonal Lenten and Advent reflection groups. All are welcome regardless of experience.

Bible Study — Sunday mornings after 9:00 AM Mass
Men's Faith Group — First Saturday of each month
Women's Prayer Circle — Wednesday evenings
Seasonal reflection groups — Lent and Advent
Our Groups
Bible Study
Sunday mornings after 9 AM Mass

A friendly, discussion-based exploration of Sunday's Scripture readings. No prior biblical knowledge required — just an open mind and a willingness to share. Led by Sofia Morales, this group welcomes everyone from lifelong Catholics to those just beginning to explore the faith.

Men's Faith Group
First Saturday of each month · 8:00 AM

A relaxed gathering of men of all ages over coffee and conversation. Each month focuses on a different theme — fatherhood, work, prayer, service — grounded in Catholic teaching. A great way to build brotherhood and accountability in faith.

Women's Prayer Circle
Wednesday evenings · 7:00 PM

A warm, supportive group of women who gather weekly for shared prayer, reflection, and friendship. The group rotates through the Rosary, Lectio Divina, and guided meditation. New members are always warmly welcomed.

Lenten Reflection Group
Weekly during Lent · Tuesday evenings

A seasonal group running through the six weeks of Lent. Each week follows a structured reflection on the Sunday Gospel, paired with practical suggestions for fasting, prayer, and almsgiving. Perfect for those who want to make Lent more intentional.

Contact: Sofia Morales, Faith Formation

Liturgical Ministries

Serve at Mass as an altar server, lector, Eucharistic minister, or usher. These roles are the backbone of our liturgical life and are open to all confirmed Catholics. Training is provided for all new ministers.

Available roles
Altar Server
Sunday Mass · training provided

Assist the priest at Mass by carrying the cross and candles, preparing the altar, and helping with the sacred vessels. Altar servers are a visible and honoured part of our liturgical life. Full training is provided for all new servers.

What we are looking for
Baptised and received First Communion
Available for at least one Mass per week
Ability to commit to a training programme
Open to middle school age and above
Lector
Proclaim Scripture at Mass

Proclaim the Word of God at Mass by reading the First and Second Readings from the Ambo. Lectors are trained in proclamation technique, pronunciation, and the theology of the Liturgy of the Word. A powerful and visible ministry.

What we are looking for
Confident reader with clear speaking voice
Willing to attend a preparation session
Confirmed Catholic in good standing
Available for at least two Masses per month
Eucharistic Minister
Must be confirmed Catholic

Assist in the distribution of Holy Communion at Mass. Extraordinary Ministers of Holy Communion also bring Communion to the sick and homebound. One of the most sacred and trusted roles in parish ministry.

What we are looking for
Confirmed Catholic in good standing
Completed a diocesan training programme (we can arrange)
Available for at least two Masses per month
Willingness to also serve the homebound preferred
Usher / Greeter
Sunday mornings · welcoming role

Ushers serve at Mass by welcoming parishioners, directing seating, taking up the collection, and maintaining order and safety in the church. Often the first or last person a visitor interacts with — a ministry of hospitality.

What we are looking for
Reliable and punctual
Available for at least one Mass per week
Comfortable with crowd management
Must be a practicing Catholic
Contact: Parish Office

Music Ministry

Join our parish choir or one of our smaller music groups. Singers and instrumentalists of all levels are welcome. Choir rehearsals are held on Thursday evenings at 7:00 PM in the Main Church.

Available roles
Choir Singer — Soprano/Alto
Thursday rehearsals 7 PM + Sunday

Join our soprano or alto section in the parish choir. The choir sings at the Sunday 11 AM Mass and for special liturgies throughout the year including Christmas, Holy Week, and the Easter Vigil. A joyful and close-knit community.

What we are looking for
Ability to read music is helpful but not required
Available Thursday evenings 7–9 PM for rehearsal
Available Sunday mornings by 10:15 AM
Willingness to learn a wide repertoire from chant to contemporary
Choir Singer — Tenor/Bass
Thursday rehearsals 7 PM + Sunday

Join our tenor or bass section in the parish choir. We are especially in need of male voices. The choir sings at the Sunday 11 AM Mass and for special liturgies. Tenors and basses make the whole choir shine.

What we are looking for
Ability to read music is helpful but not required
Available Thursday evenings 7–9 PM for rehearsal
Available Sunday mornings by 10:15 AM
Male voices particularly welcomed
Instrumentalist
Guitar, piano, violin, brass welcome

Share your musical gifts in service of the liturgy. We welcome guitarists, pianists, violinists, brass players, and other instrumentalists to play at weekend Masses and special celebrations. Skill level should be at least intermediate.

What we are looking for
Intermediate or advanced playing ability
Instrument must be suitable for liturgical use
Availability for at least one Mass per month
Willingness to coordinate repertoire with the Music Director
Cantor / Soloist
Lead congregational singing at Mass

Lead the congregation in sung prayer as a cantor at Mass. Cantors sing the Psalm, lead the assembly in the hymns and acclamations, and help create an atmosphere of prayerful worship. A ministry that combines musical skill with spiritual leadership.

What we are looking for
Strong, confident singing voice
Ability to read music
Comfortable leading a congregation
Previous liturgical cantor experience preferred but not essential
Contact: David Chen, Director of Music

Passion for working with teenagers
Practicing Catholic preferred
Retreat Chaperone
Occasional weekends · background check

Accompany youth on overnight retreat weekends as a chaperone and companion. Retreats are some of the most transformative experiences in a young person's faith journey — your presence makes them possible.

What we are looking for

Background check and Safe Environment training required
Available for occasional overnight weekends
Previous youth ministry experience helpful
Sunday School Helper
Sunday mornings during 9 AM Mass

Help our religious education teachers during the Sunday 9 AM Mass hour. Sunday School Helpers assist with classroom activities, accompany children, and support the lead teacher. A wonderful introduction to faith formation ministry.

What we are looking for

Available Sunday mornings 9–10:15 AM
Background check required
No teaching experience necessary — full support provided
Youth Events Coordinator
Plan & run youth social events

Plan and organise youth social events including game nights, service days, and seasonal gatherings. Work alongside the Youth Coordinator to create memorable experiences that build community among our young parishioners.

What we are looking for
Creative and organised
Experience with event planning a plus
Good communication skills
Passion for building community among young people

Community Outreach

Serve our neighbours through the parish food pantry, visits to the homebound, and our partnership with local social services. Volunteer shifts are available every Saturday morning and on weekday evenings.

Available roles
Food Pantry Volunteer
Saturday 9 AM–12 PM · weekly

Our food pantry serves hundreds of families each week. Volunteers help sort and pack donations, manage inventory, and assist clients with dignity and care. One of the most direct ways to live the Gospel in our neighbourhood.

What we are looking for
Available Saturday mornings 9 AM–12 PM
Able to be on your feet for 2–3 hours
Comfortable working in a fast-paced environment
Spanish language skills a significant bonus
Homebound Visitor
Visit elderly & sick parishioners

Visit elderly and housebound parishioners who cannot attend Mass. Bring them Communion, spend time in conversation, and let them know they are still part of our parish family. One of the most tender and important ministries we have.

What we are looking for
Compassionate and patient
Reliable availability on a regular schedule (weekly or fortnightly)
Comfortable visiting care homes and private homes
Trained Eucharistic Minister status required (we can arrange training)
Refugee Support
English tutoring & resettlement help

Support newly arrived refugee and immigrant families in our community. Volunteers assist with English tutoring, navigating city services, and practical resettlement tasks. This ministry is particularly needed among our Central American community.

What we are looking for
Patience and cultural sensitivity
Spanish language skills strongly preferred
Available weekday evenings or weekend mornings
No professional qualifications required — just willingness to help
Fundraising & Awareness
Organise charity drives & campaigns

Help organise fundraising drives, awareness campaigns, and donor communication for our outreach programmes. If you have skills in marketing, communications, or events, this is a great way to put them to use for a good cause.

What we are looking for
Organised and creative
Experience in communications, marketing, or fundraising a plus
Good writing skills helpful
Comfortable using social media and email
Contact: Deacon Robert Chen

Ministry

Faith Formation

Teach religious education to children, assist with RCIA, or lead an adult study group. No formal theology background is required — just a love of the faith and a willingness to share it.

Available roles
Children's RE Teacher
Sunday mornings · Sept–May

Teach religious education to children from Kindergarten through Grade 8 on Sunday mornings during the school year. Full curriculum and resources are provided. No formal theology degree required — just a love of the faith and a willingness to share it.

What we are looking for
Practicing Catholic in good standing
Comfortable speaking to a group of children
Background check required
Willing to complete a short orientation programme before September
RCIA Sponsor
Walk with a candidate through initiation

Walk alongside an RCIA candidate through their journey of initiation into the Catholic Church. A sponsor attends weekly sessions with their candidate, answers questions, shares their own faith story, and supports them through to Easter Vigil.

What we are looking for
Confirmed Catholic with active parish life
Able to attend weekly RCIA sessions from October to Easter
Willingness to share openly about your own faith journey
No formal theology background required
Adult Study Group Leader
Lead weekly faith discussions

Facilitate weekly adult faith formation discussions using provided materials. Groups meet in parishioners' homes or the parish hall and cover topics from Scripture to Catholic social teaching. Leaders do not need to be experts — just good listeners.

What we are looking for
Comfortable facilitating group discussion
Practicing Catholic with a love of learning
Reliable availability for weekly meetings
Able to complete a brief leader orientation
Confirmation Mentor
Guide a teen through Confirmation prep

Be paired with a teenage Confirmation candidate as their personal mentor through the two-year preparation programme. Meet monthly, attend sessions together, and offer support and example as they prepare to receive the Holy Spirit.

What we are looking for
Confirmed Catholic in good standing
Background check required
Able to meet monthly with your candidate for two years
Comfortable talking about faith with a teenager
Contact: Sofia Morales, Director of Faith Formation
