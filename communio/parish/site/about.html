For over 135 years, St. Mary's has been a beacon of faith and community in New York's Lower East Side. We are a diverse Catholic community that welcomes all people to experience the love of Christ.

Our Heritage

Founded on faith, built by community

In 1887, a small group of Catholic immigrants from Ireland, Italy, and Eastern Europe established St. Mary's Parish in the heart of Manhattan's Lower East Side. What began as weekly Masses in a rented storefront has grown into a thriving parish community.

Through two world wars, the Great Depression, urban renewal, and the changing face of New York City, St. Mary's has remained a constant source of hope, comfort, and spiritual guidance for generations of families.

Today, we continue that legacy — welcoming all who seek Christ, serving our neighbors in need, and building a more just and compassionate world, one soul at a time.

Our Journey

Key moments in the life of our parish

1887
Parish Founded

St. Mary's Parish established by Irish and Italian immigrant families, holding first Mass in a rented storefront on Rivington Street.

1902
Church Building Completed

Construction of our beautiful Gothic Revival church completed, featuring stained glass windows crafted in Ireland.

1925
School Opens

St. Mary's Elementary School opens its doors, serving the children of the Lower East Side for over 60 years.

1965
Vatican II Renewal

Parish embraces liturgical reforms of Vatican II, adding guitar Mass and increasing lay participation in ministry.

1980s
Immigration Sanctuary

St. Mary's becomes a sanctuary for Central American refugees, offering legal aid and resettlement services.

2001
9/11 Response

Parish serves as refuge and healing center for Lower Manhattan community in the aftermath of September 11th attacks.

2020
Pandemic Adaptation

Launches online Masses, contactless food distribution, and virtual faith formation during COVID-19 pandemic.

Today
Growing Forward

Continues to welcome newcomers from around the world while serving the changing needs of our neighborhood.
