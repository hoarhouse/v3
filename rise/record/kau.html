What It Is

KAÜ (Központi Azonosítási Ügynök — Central Authentication Agent) is Hungary's national digital authorisation service. It is the system that authenticates millions of Hungarian citizens when they access government digital services — from tax submissions to healthcare records to administrative portals.

KAÜ is the identity backbone of Hungary's digital government. It is not a minor component of the country's digital infrastructure — it is the trust layer that everything else depends on.

E-Group's Role

KAÜ was designed and implemented personally by András Nagy, Co-founder and CTO of E-Group. This is not a project E-Group contributed to as one of many participants — András Nagy architected the system, led its technical implementation, and built it from the ground up.

The egroup.hu management page lists this specifically in András Nagy's professional record: "Designed and implemented E-Government (KAÜ, Hungarian State digital authorization service) in Hungary."

András also managed the South East Asian State Digital Authorization Service — demonstrating that E-Group's identity infrastructure expertise extends well beyond Hungary.

KAÜ sits within E-Group's broader identity stack for the Ministry of Interior and NISZ — a partnership continuous since 2003, spanning modules including JKÜ, AVDH, SZEÜSZ, Hun-PEPS, biometric onboarding, and cloud HSM. See the full Ministry of Interior record for the complete scope.

The Significance

Building national digital identity infrastructure at this scale requires a depth of expertise in cryptography, PKI, digital signatures, authentication protocols, and government-grade security that almost no private company possesses. E-Group built this when most European countries were still debating whether digital identity was viable.

This work is the direct foundation for E-Group's involvement in eIDAS 2.0 and the European Digital Identity Wallet Consortium. The MyD Wallet explicitly maintains backward compatibility with KAÜ — the two systems are architecturally linked.

Academic Roots

The cryptographic foundations for this work trace back to 2001, when an E-Group-affiliated researcher began exploring electronic signature legislation at the BME research laboratory. This academic-to-production pipeline — from university research to national infrastructure — is characteristic of how E-Group operates.

Deployment Scope

E-Group clients related to this work include NISZ (National IT Services), OEP (National Health Insurance Fund), Ministry of Interior, and the National Ambulance Service, among others.

2022 Security Enhancement

In June 2022, E-Group's two-factor authentication (2FA) solution was implemented on the KAÜ platform, providing TOTP-based (Time-based One-Time Password) security for millions of government service users. This enhancement affects access to major Hungarian e-government services including NAV, EESZT, and all KAÜ-connected systems.

The 2FA implementation represents a significant security upgrade, requiring users to provide both traditional credentials and time-limited mobile device codes for authentication, substantially reducing automated abuse and unauthorized access risks.
