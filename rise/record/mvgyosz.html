What It Is

Remote Eye — Távszem in Hungarian — is a real-time visual assistance service for blind and partially sighted users in Hungary. It connects visually impaired users with sighted volunteers through a mobile app, letting the volunteer see what the user's camera sees and help with visual recognition tasks in real time.

It is built and operated by E-Group in partnership with MVGYOSZ — Magyar Vakok és Gyengénlátók Országos Szövetsége, the National Association for the Blind and Partially Sighted.

What It Does

When a visually impaired user needs help — reading a label, identifying an object, navigating an unfamiliar space, or any task where sight is required — they open the app. The platform routes the call to an available sighted volunteer via live video. The volunteer sees what the user's camera sees and helps them through the task.

The platform runs on E-Group's MOBIX Communication Platform, part of the Xchange product line — the same infrastructure family used for other mission-critical real-time communication services.

Core capabilities:

  • Volunteer-to-user matching with live video
  • Safe, reliable streaming technology built for 24/7 availability
  • Call-centre integration and queue management
  • Encrypted communication channels
  • GDPR-compliant and independently certified

Recognition and Impact

Remote Eye was awarded the 2020 Audience Award for Social Innovation — recognition earned from the public, not granted by institutions. Over 5,000 registered users rely on the service.

This is part of a broader E-Group pattern: building public-interest technology that has to actually work, for real people, every day, without interruption.
