The Problem

AI-generated imagery is now
indistinguishable to the human eye.

In 2024, the gap between a real photograph and an AI-generated image closed. Not partially. Completely. A skilled professional cannot reliably tell them apart. Neither can a judge. Neither can an insurance assessor. Neither can a journalist.

This creates a fundamental trust problem across every industry that depends on photographic evidence. Insurance claims. Legal proceedings. Media verification. Facility inspections. Drone-based asset management. The entire evidentiary value of photography — built over 180 years — is now in question.

ProofX is E-Group's answer to that question. Not a filter. Not a detection algorithm. A cryptographic proof — built at the moment of capture — that establishes an unbreakable chain of evidence between a photograph and the physical reality it records.

The Technical Foundation
  • Galileo OSNMA — ESA's Open Service Navigation Message Authentication. Cryptographic satellite timestamps embedded at the exact moment of capture.
  • C2PA Standard — Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity. End-to-end tamper-proof metadata chain from capture to publication.
  • eIDAS Identity — EU-compliant digital identity of the image creator. AML-grade KYC authentication.
  • EU AI Act — Compliant real-or-AI transparency layer. Every image carries a verifiable provenance declaration.
How It Works

A cryptographic chain.
Built at the moment of capture.

ProofX does not analyse images after the fact. It creates a cryptographic proof at the exact second of capture — binding the image to a satellite-verified location, a satellite-verified time, and a verified identity. That proof travels with the image, permanently, and cannot be forged.

Galileo OSNMA Authentication
At the moment of capture, ProofX records a cryptographic timestamp from the Galileo satellite constellation using ESA's OSNMA authentication protocol. This binds the image to a specific location and time that cannot be retrospectively altered.
C2PA Metadata Chain
The image is wrapped in a C2PA-compliant metadata structure that records every modification, every export, every share. Any tampering with the image breaks the chain — and that break is immediately detectable by any C2PA-aware system.
eIDAS Creator Identity
The identity of the person or system that captured the image is bound to the proof using eIDAS-compliant digital identity. Court-admissible. AML-grade KYC. The image knows who took it and can prove it.
Court-Admissible Output
The resulting evidence chain is designed to meet the evidential standards of European courts and insurance regulations. Not a best-effort approximation — a formal proof structure with defined legal standing.
Airlot S1 Drone Integration
ProofX is optimised for the Airlot S1 drone — enabling tamper-proof aerial intelligence. Every frame captured by the drone carries a full cryptographic proof of its origin, time, and location.
EU AI Act Compliance
ProofX generates a real-or-AI transparency declaration for every image — compliant with EU AI Act requirements for synthetic media disclosure. Every image either carries proof of real-world capture or is declared synthetic.

The ESA Partnership

Built with the people
who own the satellites.

ProofX is being developed in active partnership with the European Space Agency. ESA's Galileo constellation provides the OSNMA authentication layer — the cryptographic heartbeat that makes the entire proof chain possible.

This is not a licensing arrangement. E-Group is working directly with ESA on the technical implementation of Galileo OSNMA for commercial authentication applications. ProofX is one of the first commercial products to build on this capability.

The partnership reflects E-Group's broader position in the European space technology ecosystem — as a technology builder, not a reseller of someone else's platform.
