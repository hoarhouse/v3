What It Is

Remote Eye is an accessibility platform that provides 24/7 remote assistance for visually impaired users across Hungary. Through a mobile application, users connect instantly with trained call-center operators who provide visual assistance for daily tasks — reading documents, navigating spaces, identifying objects, or any situation where sight is needed.

The platform is built on E-Group's MOBIX Communication Platform, part of the Xchange product line. This isn't a proof of concept or pilot — Remote Eye has been operational since July 2019, serving thousands of users daily with round-the-clock availability.

Live Status and Impact

Remote Eye has been live in production since July 2019, providing uninterrupted service for over five years. The platform represents a nationwide accessibility service expansion, making visual assistance available to any visually impaired person in Hungary with a smartphone.

  • Thousands of daily active users
  • 24/7 service availability — no downtime, no service windows
  • Trained operators providing professional visual assistance
  • Full mobile accessibility features integrated

Technical Foundation

Remote Eye is powered by MOBIX Communication Platform, E-Group's enterprise-grade communication infrastructure. The platform handles real-time video streaming, secure connections, and call-center integration at scale. Key technical capabilities include:

  • Real-time video streaming with low latency
  • Call-center integration and operator management
  • Mobile accessibility features (screen readers, voice commands)
  • Secure, encrypted communication channels
  • Scalable infrastructure supporting thousands of concurrent users

Client and Partnership

Remote Eye was developed for the National Association of the Hungarian Blind and Visually Impaired, Hungary's primary advocacy and service organization for the visually impaired community. The association represents over 50,000 visually impaired individuals across the country.

This partnership reflects E-Group's commitment to applying enterprise-grade technology to social impact projects — using the same infrastructure that powers banking systems and government services to enable accessibility at national scale.
