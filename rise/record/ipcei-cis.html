What It Is

The IPCEI CIS (Important Project of Common European Interest — Next Generation Cloud Infrastructure and Services) is the EU's flagship sovereign cloud and AI infrastructure programme. It was jointly submitted by seven member states — France, Germany, Hungary, Italy, the Netherlands, Poland, and Spain — and approved by the European Commission in December 2023.

E-Group's project within IPCEI-CIS is called FedEU.ai. It addresses the core challenge of AI at European scale: ensuring the security, sovereignty, and trustworthiness of massive data access and processing — without depending on US hyperscalers.

E-Group's Role

Of the 19 enterprises selected across all 7 member states, E-Group is one of only two Hungarian companies (alongside 4iG Nyrt.) to receive IPCEI certification. Within the programme structure:

  • E-Group co-leads Workstream 3 (WS3) — responsible for advanced modular processing services for the Multi-Provider Cloud-Edge Continuum
  • E-Group leads the AI Cluster within WS3 — the working group developing intelligent and advanced data processing capabilities across all participating partners
  • Dr. Péter Antal serves as FedEU.ai's lead researcher, applying his expertise in federated learning to the core technical architecture

What FedEU.ai Builds

FedEU.ai aims to create a scalable, cloud-native federated learning system and software framework that ensures the reliability of both data and AI models while applying advanced security and data protection protocols.

Key technical outcomes include: reduced AI model development costs through federated approaches; new business models for data reuse that allow data providers to monetise without exposing raw data; and a proprietary protocol that reduces the environmental footprint of large-scale federated machine learning by up to 25%.

WS3 Partners

E-Group hosted the IPCEI-CIS/8RA Workshop at its Budapest headquarters in November 2025, bringing together WS3 partners including CloudFerro, ENEA, Engineering, Gdańsk University of Technology, Infobip, Reply, Result, Tiscali, and TIM Group for technical alignment and system integration planning.

Recognition

Following IPCEI qualification, State Secretary Gergely Fábián (responsible for industry policies and technology) personally welcomed E-Group CEO Antal Kuthy and Deputy CEO László Blénessy at his office — a formal government recognition of E-Group's role in Hungary's sovereign digital infrastructure strategy.

Within 30 days of appearing at the IPCEI Forum, E-Group's FedLab-X product attracted business interest from nearly 30 major companies.
