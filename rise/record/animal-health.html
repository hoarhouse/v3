What It Is

A 3-year government-funded R&D project with the University of Veterinary Medicine Budapest — Europe's oldest veterinary university with almost 250 years of tradition, listed among the top 7 European veterinary schools. The project launched on October 1, 2021 with funding from the Hungarian National Research, Development and Innovation Office.

The mission: tackle the threatening global rise of antimicrobial resistance in animal medicine by building a veterinary drug repositioning platform on E-Group's data infrastructure.

One Health Approach

The project employs the One Health approach — a collaborative, multisectoral methodology that recognises the interconnection between animal health, veterinary public health, food safety, and human health. This integrated perspective is essential for addressing antibiotic resistance, which affects the entire food chain and public health ecosystem.

By combining data from these traditionally siloed domains, the platform creates comprehensive insights that would be impossible to achieve through conventional single-domain approaches.

Mission and Impact

The platform identifies candidate agents that can be used as feed additives — compounds which either reduce antibiotic resistance directly, or act in synergy with existing antibiotics to increase their effectiveness. Either path reduces overall antibiotic usage across the veterinary food chain.

Rather than developing entirely new antibiotics — a process that can take decades — the project focuses on discovering new veterinary applications for existing pharmaceutical compounds. This significantly accelerates the timeline for bringing effective treatments to market while reducing development costs and regulatory complexity.

Technical Foundation

Built on blueprints from E-Group's successful InnoHealth DataLake project, the Animal Health Innovation Platform applies proven data engineering methodology to the specific requirements of veterinary and food safety data.

  • Drug-repositioning analysis driven by gene expression profiling
  • Data lake infrastructure for integrating heterogeneous One Health data sources
  • European data standards compliance
  • Secure and privacy-assuring central and local IT infrastructure
  • Assessment and data harmonisation activities across veterinary and public health domains
  • Testing and maintenance support

The platform represents a foundation for broader food chain safety data initiatives, positioning E-Group at the intersection of veterinary medicine, public health, and data science.
