> Working document. Last updated April 2026. Owner: Chris Hoar / Tibor.

---

## The North Star

E-Group built the infrastructure AI needs before anyone knew AI would need it. Thirty years of delivery. Now the world has caught up.

---

## The Character

Quiet confidence. A slight raised eyebrow at the noise. E-Group has been solving real problems for real institutions while others were busy announcing they were about to solve them.

The work is serious. The voice can afford to be dry.

We are the antithesis of tech bro culture — the self-promotion, the disruption theatre, the moving fast and breaking things that belong to other people. E-Group moves carefully because the things it builds cannot be broken and patched later. National identity systems, energy infrastructure, health data platforms. Real people depend on this. That matters.

---

## How We Write

- Short sentences. Active voice. Present tense where possible.
- State facts. Don't decorate them.
- No adjectives that don't earn their place.
- If you can cut a word without losing meaning, cut it.
- Dry wit is welcome. Smugness is not.
- Humour comes from contrast — what others claim vs what E-Group actually delivered.

| There's a difference | We believe |

---

## How We Name Things

Direct. One or two words. No corporate wrapper. Names that assume the reader is intelligent.

**Current nav:**
- The Record — not "Case Studies" or "Portfolio"
- The Work — not "Products" or "Solutions"
- What's Next — not "Pipeline" or "Roadmap"
- Thinking — not "Insights" or "Blog"
- Work With Us — not "Contact" or "Get in Touch"

**Rule:** If the name needs explaining, it's the wrong name.

---

## The One Test

Before publishing anything, ask: would a senior partner at a serious European institution read this and feel like E-Group is the kind of company they want to work with?

If yes — publish. If no — rewrite.
