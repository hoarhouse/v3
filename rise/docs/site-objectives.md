# E-Group Website — Site Objectives & Audience

> Working document. Last updated April 2026. Owner: Chris Hoar / Tibor.

---

## Primary Audiences

These are the ONLY two audiences that matter right now. Everything on the site is written for them.

### 1 — Existing Customers
Companies and institutions that already work with E-Group. They know who we are. They trust us. The site needs to deepen that relationship — show them what else we've built, what we're thinking, where we're going. Give them reasons to stay engaged and reasons to share us with their networks.

### 2 — The Warm Circle
Companies in our consortium memberships, trade groups, and EU initiative partnerships. IPCEI-CIS partners, HeliX consortium members, DOME participants, EWC members. They know of us. They've seen us at the table. The site needs to convert that familiarity into genuine interest — make them want to work with us, partner with us, or refer us.

### What this site is NOT for
Cold acquisition. Random traffic. SEO-driven strangers. We are not trying to rank for "AI company Budapest." We are building a relationship platform for people who already have reason to pay attention.

---

## Site Objectives

In order of priority:

### 1 — Build reputation and brand
Show who E-Group really is. Not a startup. Not a consultant. A thirty-year company that built the infrastructure AI needs before anyone knew AI would need it. Every page should make a sophisticated visitor think "I didn't know they did that."

### 2 — Build community
Give our warm circle a reason to come back. Fresh thinking. Honest opinions. A window into what E-Group is building and why. The Thinking section is the engine of this. Events, consortium updates, and momentum signals feed it.

### 3 — Generate partnerships and sales
The site is not a sales funnel. It is a trust builder that makes the sales conversation easier. A warm circle CTO who has read three Thinking articles and seen the MAVIR and ESA credentials before they take a call is a completely different conversation to a cold prospect.

---

## What Success Looks Like

- Warm circle contacts visit and share pages with colleagues
- Existing customers feel proud to be associated with E-Group
- Inbound partnership enquiries reference something specific they saw on the site
- The site feels alive — updated, opinionated, worth returning to

---

## What Failure Looks Like

- A static site that never changes
- Generic copy that could apply to any technology company
- Pages that exist but say nothing worth reading
- Visitors who leave without understanding what makes E-Group different
