NEMESIS

Federated Procurement Integrity Network

Cross-border fraud detection without cross-border data sharing

Nemesis is a federated procurement fraud detection platform that enables anti-corruption agencies across the EU to identify cross-border bid-rigging, collusion networks, and kickback schemes—without sharing sensitive national procurement data. Built on E-Group's proven federated architecture, Nemesis lets investigators coordinate across borders while data stays home.

Public procurement fraud costs the EU €5B+ annually, but catching it is nearly impossible. Why? Because fraud networks operate across borders—the same shell companies bidding in Romania, Bulgaria, and Hungary—while procurement data stays locked in national silos. Sharing databases would expose sensitive government contracts and create unacceptable security risks. So fraudsters coordinate freely while investigators work blind.

Nemesis changes this equation with federated pattern detection. Our models analyze bidding patterns, company networks, and pricing anomalies across multiple national databases simultaneously—without any procurement data ever leaving national systems. When suspicious patterns emerge in one country, the network checks for connections everywhere. Fraudsters can no longer hide behind borders.

The Invisible Corruption Problem

Public procurement accounts for 14% of EU GDP, yet corruption drains billions annually. The problem? Fraud patterns span borders, but data stays local.

Siloed Detection

Each country analyzes only its own tenders. Cross-border bid-rigging rings operate invisibly across jurisdictions.

Data Sovereignty Barriers

GDPR and national laws prevent sharing sensitive bidder information. Legitimate compliance creates detection blind spots.

Sophisticated Schemes

Shell company networks, rotating bid patterns, artificial specifications - today's fraud is designed to evade single-nation analysis.

Delayed Discovery

Average time to detect procurement fraud: 2+ years. By then, funds are gone and perpetrators have moved on.
