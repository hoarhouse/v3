ALETHEIA is the EU's first federated professional credential verification network. We enable employers, regulators, and institutions to instantly verify degrees, professional licenses, and certifications across all 27 EU member states—without centralizing sensitive personal data or violating GDPR.

Credential fraud is a €10B+ problem destroying trust in professional qualifications. A surgeon licensed in Munich can't easily prove their credentials in Milan. Universities spend months manually verifying transcripts. And 23% of CVs contain falsified credentials that current systems miss entirely. The result: patients treated by fake doctors, buildings designed by unlicensed engineers, and financial advice from fraudulent advisors.

ALETHEIA solves this with federated verification. When you need to verify a credential, our network queries the issuing institution directly—the university, licensing board, or certification body. The verification happens at the source, results return in seconds, and no centralized database of qualifications ever exists. Privacy preserved, fraud eliminated, mobility enabled.

Cross-Border Credential Verification Is Broken

Bureaucratic Nightmare

Verifying a doctor who trained in Spain, practiced in France, and wants to work in Germany requires contacting 3 different authorities, in 3 languages, with different forms, fees, and response times. Average: 3-6 months.

Fraud Is Invisible

Each licensing body only sees their own registry. A credential revoked in one country is still valid everywhere else. Fraudsters exploit these gaps - fake degrees, forged licenses, stolen identities.

No One Will Centralize

Creating a central EU database of all professionals is politically impossible. Licensing bodies won't surrender control of their registries. GDPR makes cross-border data sharing legally complex.

Workforce Mobility Demands Speed

17M+ EU citizens work in another member state. Healthcare worker shortages are critical. The current verification bottleneck costs lives and billions in economic friction.
