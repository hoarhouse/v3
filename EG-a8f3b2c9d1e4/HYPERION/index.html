HYPERION

Identity & Security for Next-Generation Light-Based Computing

HYPERION provides identity verification and security controls for photonic computers—a new type of processor that uses light instead of electricity to perform calculations.

What is photonic computing?

Traditional computers use electrical signals. Photonic computers use light particles (photons), making them dramatically faster and more energy-efficient. Companies like Lightmatter, Q.ANT, and Lumai are bringing these systems to market now, with major applications in AI, defense, and scientific research.

The problem HYPERION solves:

When organizations connect photonic processors into networks for AI training or sensitive computations, they need to answer critical questions:

  • Which computing nodes are legitimate and authorized?
  • Who owns each node, and where is it physically located?
  • What data is each node allowed to access?
  • Who is accountable if something goes wrong?

What HYPERION does:

  • Verifies each photonic node's identity with EU-certified digital credentials
  • Controls what each node can access with real-time permission enforcement
  • Tracks every operation with immutable audit logs for regulatory compliance
  • Ensures EU data sovereignty so your compute infrastructure doesn't depend on US cloud services

Think of HYPERION as the 'passport and security clearance system' for photonic computing nodes—verifying who they are, what they're allowed to do, and keeping records of everything.

Built on E-Group's eIDAS 2.0 certified MyD Wallet, HYPERION is the first sovereign identity infrastructure designed specifically for photonic AI systems. As Europe invests billions in photonic computing through IPCEI initiatives, HYPERION ensures sovereignty isn't sacrificed for speed.
