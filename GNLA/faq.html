Everything you need to know about the Assembly, the Rome Declaration, and how to participate.

About the Assembly
What is the Global Nobel Laureates Assembly?
+
The Global Nobel Laureates Assembly on Artificial Intelligence and Nuclear War is a high-level international convening bringing together Nobel Laureates, AI scientists, economists, and global leaders to address the intersection of artificial intelligence and nuclear risk. The Assembly will take place at Borgo Laudato Si', Castel Gandolfo, Vatican, on 14–16 July 2026, and will result in the Rome Declaration.
Who organizes the Assembly?
+
The Assembly is convened by nine global institutions: the Nobel Laureates Assembly for the Prevention of Nuclear War, the University of Chicago Existential Risk Laboratory, International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War (IPPNW), the University of Notre Dame Kroc Institute, Soka Gakkai International, the Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists, Harvard Medical School, Pugwash Conferences, and the Domus Communis Foundation, which serves as General Secretariat.
Is this event affiliated with the Vatican or the Pope?
+
The Assembly is hosted at Borgo Laudato Si', an international ecological center located within Vatican territory at the Pontifical Villas of Castel Gandolfo. The venue is operated by the Laudato Si' Movement and is not a Vatican event per se. However, the choice of venue reflects the moral and spiritual resonance of the Assembly's mission, and the Laudato Si' framework is directly aligned with the Assembly's vision of integral human development.
What is the Rome Declaration?
+
The Rome Declaration on an Unarmed and Disarming Peace in the Age of Artificial Intelligence is the principal outcome of the Assembly. It will be a strategic and normative document articulating the principles of a renewed global security framework — addressing AI governance in military contexts, nuclear risk reduction, and the vision of a security paradigm grounded in cooperation and human dignity rather than deterrence and fear.
Attending
Who can attend the Assembly?
+
The Assembly has a restricted attendance policy. Most sessions are closed to invited participants only. However, several afternoon sessions are open to accredited media, and the global press conference on 16 July is open to press and public. Individuals wishing to attend may submit a request via the Accept Invitation page — requests are reviewed by the Organizing Committee.
Is there a live stream?
+
Yes. Open sessions and the global press conference on 16 July will be available via live stream. Access details will be provided to all subscribers and registered online followers. Sign up via the Accept Invitation page.
How do I apply for press accreditation?
+
Accredited journalists, photographers, and broadcast media can apply via the Accept Invitation page (Press Accreditation tab) or by contacting press@domuscommunis.org. The deadline for press credential applications is 30 June 2026.
What languages will sessions be conducted in?
+
The primary language of the Assembly is English. Simultaneous interpretation into Italian will be provided for open sessions. Additional language support may be available — details will be confirmed closer to the event.
How do I get to Castel Gandolfo?
+
Castel Gandolfo is located approximately 25 kilometres south of Rome. It is accessible by regional train from Roma Termini (approximately 25 minutes to Albano Laziale station, then a short taxi or bus). By car, the journey from Rome centre takes approximately 35 minutes, and from Fiumicino airport approximately 45 minutes via the GRA ring road.
Can individuals and organizations endorse the Rome Declaration?
+
Following the Assembly and the public release of the Rome Declaration on 16 July 2026, a formal endorsement process will be opened for institutions, organizations, and individuals worldwide. Register your interest via the Follow Online option on the Accept Invitation page to be notified when this process opens.
