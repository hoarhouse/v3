Programme

Three days of
deliberation & action

Structured to combine the depth of closed scientific deliberation with the reach of high-level public engagement — culminating in the Rome Declaration.

1
14 July 2026
Understanding the Systemic Shift
Morning
Opening Remarks & Institutional Salutations
Formal opening of the Assembly by convening institutions and members of the Organizing Committee.
Closed
Morning
The AI–Nuclear Convergence: A Systemic Risk
Exploring how artificial intelligence is transforming nuclear systems, decision-making, and global strategic stability. With Nobel Laureates, leading AI scientists, and senior nuclear security experts.
Closed
Morning
Speed, Automation and the Risk of Miscalculation
AI-driven systems, compressed timelines, and the erosion of human control in high-risk environments. Global technology leaders and defense experts.
Closed
Afternoon
The Consequences of Nuclear Use in the Age of AI
Humanitarian, environmental, and systemic impacts of nuclear conflict in a technologically interconnected world.
Open to Media
Afternoon
From an Economy of War to an Economy of Peace
Rethinking global economic structures in light of technological transformation. With Nobel Laureates in Economics and global financial leaders.
Open to Media
Evening
A Shared Responsibility: Ethics, Humanity and the Future
A moderated evening dialogue among faith leaders, Nobel Laureates, and global thought leaders.
Invitation Only
2
15 July 2026
Governance, Opportunity and New Frameworks
Morning
Governing AI in High-Risk Systems
Toward new models of governance for artificial intelligence in strategic, military, and critical infrastructures. Leading AI policy experts and institutional representatives.
Closed
Morning
New Digital Protocols and Strategic Stability
Defining the need for shared protocols governing AI, data sovereignty, and decision-making in global systems.
Open to Media
Afternoon
New Models of Digital Development
AI as a driver of new economic systems, data sovereignty, and inclusive global development. Philanthropists, technology entrepreneurs, and development finance leaders.
Open to Media
Afternoon
Building a Global Platform: From Dialogue to Action
Translating reflection into coordinated global action across institutions, technology, and society.
Open to Media
3
16 July 2026
Toward the Rome Declaration
Morning
Closing Deliberations
Final closed deliberation among Nobel Laureates, institutional representatives, and members of the Steering Committee.
Invitation Only
Afternoon
Presentation of the Rome Declaration
Presentation and discussion of key principles emerging from the Assembly — the founding text of a renewed global security framework.
Invitation Only
Afternoon
Global Press Conference
Public announcement and presentation of the Rome Declaration by Nobel Laureates and Assembly leadership. Broadcast globally.
