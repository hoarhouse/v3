The Setting

Borgo Laudato Si'
Castel Gandolfo

The traditional summer residence of the Popes — now an international ecological center within Vatican territory, inspired by Pope Francis' encyclical Laudato si'.

The Pontifical Villas of Castel Gandolfo sit on the volcanic hills of the Alban Hills, overlooking Lake Albano, 25 kilometers southeast of Rome. For centuries, this has been the summer residence of the Popes — a place of contemplation, hospitality, and moral authority.

Today, within the grounds of the Papal Villas, the Borgo Laudato Si' has been established: an international ecological center inspired by Pope Francis' encyclical Laudato si'. The Borgo aims at creating communities that adopt ecological, social, and economic practices in line with principles of justice and solidarity.

The choice of this place is itself a statement: that the work of peace is as sacred as any other human endeavor.

Why Castel Gandolfo

The selection of Borgo Laudato Si' as the venue for the Assembly is not incidental. It reflects the conviction that the challenge of AI and nuclear risk is not merely technical or political — it is moral and civilizational. The venue carries the weight of centuries of moral reflection, and the Laudato Si' framework offers a vision of integral human development that speaks directly to the Assembly's purpose.

The natural beauty and spiritual atmosphere of the Alban Hills provide the conditions for the depth of reflection that this moment requires.

Practical Information

Castel Gandolfo is located approximately 25 minutes by train from Rome Termini, or 35 minutes by car from Rome city center and Fiumicino Airport. Detailed travel and accommodation guidance will be provided to registered participants.
