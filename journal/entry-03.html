The Fundamental Shift

We asked the wrong question first

For the first sessions, Rise was built around a simple premise: something happens, a human submits it, AI generates content. That's a useful tool. It saves time. It removes friction. But it's not what makes content worth reading.

The question we should have started with — and the one that changed everything in this session — is:

"Why would a smart, busy person stop scrolling, read this, and then share it with their network?"

The answer is never "because a company announced something." The answer is always "because it told me something I didn't know, connected to something I already care about, and sharing it makes me look informed."

That realisation rewrote the entire Rise architecture. The platform doesn't write content about eGroup. It reads the world, finds where eGroup belongs in an existing conversation, and writes content that earns its place in someone's feed.

What We Were Getting Wrong

The press release problem

Early outputs from Rise looked like this: "eGroup CEO Antal Kuthy has been confirmed as a speaker at the AI Sovereignty Summit..." Professional. Accurate. Completely forgettable. Nobody shares content that reads like a filing cabinet.

The root cause was that the AI was generating content in a vacuum. It knew the trigger — someone registered for a conference — but it didn't know what was happening in FinTech this week, what the actual conversation at Money 20/20 was about, or what specific angle would make an industry insider stop and think "yes, exactly that."

The Decision

Every Piece of Content (POC) must begin with live research — what the world is talking about right now in this domain, verified across multiple sources. No exceptions. The AI writes last, not first.

This added complexity. It added time — 30 to 60 seconds per trigger instead of 8 to 10. But it fundamentally changed the quality ceiling. Rise went from generating plausible content to generating content that has a reason to exist.

The Architecture

Six steps. Zero human input.

Rise Intelligence Pipeline — V3
01
Event Detected
Integration Layer detects the trigger from a connected business system — calendar, HR, CRM, or project management. Zero human action required.
02
Live Trend Intelligence New in V3
Rise searches the web for what the world is talking about RIGHT NOW in this domain — current debates, shifts, controversies. Not last month. This week.
03
Deep Event Research + Verification New in V3
Multiple targeted searches on the specific trigger. Sources are cross-referenced. Every fact is tagged as confirmed, single-source, or flagged before it enters a POC.
04
Rise is Thinking New in V3
Before writing a single word, Rise answers five mandatory questions: What conversation is already happening? What does eGroup uniquely know? What's the most surprising angle? Why would someone share this? What's the scroll-stopping first sentence?
05
Content Generation
Platform-specific content written using verified research, the chosen angle, mandatory engagement structures, and brand voice rules. Designed to stop the scroll. Not to fill a feed.
06
Brand Check + Review Queue
Scored against brand voice rules. Queued for admin review with full context: what Rise found, why it made the choices it did, verification status of every fact.
Key Decisions This Session

The moments that changed the product

📖
Rise reads the world before it writes
Every POC starts with live web research — not static context. This was the fundamental shift that changed the quality ceiling from "plausible" to "shareable." The timer and discovery feed make this visible.
✅
Facts are verified before they enter a POC
eGroup's credibility cannot be compromised by an inaccurate claim. A verification protocol now runs on every fact — confirmed across sources, or flagged before an admin sees it.
🎨
Brand Voice Editor — admins own the rules
The rules Rise writes by are no longer buried in code. Admins see them, edit them, and save them. Voice, what to never write, what to always write, company context. The AI follows the admin — not the other way round.
🧠
The Learning Loop — Rise gets smarter per organisation
Every approval, edit, rejection, and engagement signal feeds a model that adjusts Rise's structure over time. What works for eGroup's audience becomes Rise's default. What doesn't gets deprioritised. The moat builds itself.
📷
Visuals are first-class content
Every POC now includes an image recommendation — sourced from eGroup's asset library, the event's official imagery, or AI-generated to brief. In the demo, Unsplash provides real photographs per trigger type. In production, Rise will source from eGroup's own library first.
📐
Mandatory content structures — engineered for engagement
Each platform now has a mandatory structure. LinkedIn opens with a world-context hook, builds to eGroup's angle, ends with a genuine question. X threads have a strict 3-tweet structure — hook, substance, angle — with hashtags on the final tweet only. Website titles are capped at 7 words and must create curiosity, not summarise.
