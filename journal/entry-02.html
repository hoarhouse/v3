The Company, The Brand & The First Page

Deep dive on what eGroup actually is. The positioning decision — "Thirty years building what AI runs on." Design direction and the homepage built in one session.

Entry 01 ended with a clear mandate: build something that makes eGroup's content problem structurally impossible. But before we could build Rise, we needed to build the thing Rise will eventually run — eGroup's new website. And before we could build that, we needed to understand what eGroup actually is. Not what the old website said. What the company actually is.

That investigation changed everything about the direction we took.

"No — forget the old site. It's useless and has almost no relation to the company. Ignore everything about it, from design to style."
— Chris Hoar, Project Lead. The brief that set us free.

Understanding eGroup — The Real Company

Time was spent going deep on what eGroup actually is before touching a single line of code. What emerged was a company far more impressive than its digital presence suggests — and one standing at a genuine inflection point.

  • ✓
    Founded 1993 — 30+ years of deep tech IP. Not a startup, not a consultancy. A technology group that has been building serious infrastructure since before most of today's AI companies existed.
  • ✓
    Built Hungary's national digital identity infrastructure. The KAÜ — Hungary's central administrative identification system used by millions of citizens — was designed and built by eGroup. This is mission-critical national infrastructure.
  • ✓
    September 2025 — Sovereign AI Ecosystem MoU with Kellton. A signed agreement to jointly build a Sovereign AI Ecosystem combining European AI sovereignty with deep tech capability. Front page news, sitting completely invisible on the old website.
  • ✓
    EU DOME project — selected as Hungary's representative. Participating in the Distributed Open Marketplace for Cloud and Edge Services. eGroup leads payment subsystem development and trusted identity framework design.
  • ✓
    Medical AI — back-to-back first-place wins. The research team won consecutive competitions at international scientific events. Proof of genuine research depth, not just commercial work.
  • ✓
    Post-quantum encryption work. Active development on quantum-safe cryptographic standards — one of the most forward-looking areas in enterprise security right now.
The Critical Context

eGroup has historically operated primarily on grants, building concepts and tools for European governments. CEO Antal Kuthy now wants to build a company with products that enterprises around the world actually want to buy. This is not a rebrand. It is a fundamental transformation — and the new website is the first public signal of that intent.

The Positioning Decision

With a clear picture of what eGroup is, the positioning question became: what is the single most honest, most powerful thing we can say about this company to a CTO in Singapore, a CISO in New York, or a government CIO in Berlin?

The answer came from looking at what eGroup has that no competitor can replicate. Not a feature. Not a sector. A timeline. Thirty years of building the specific infrastructure — trust, identity, data sovereignty, transactional systems — that the entire world is suddenly desperate for now that AI has made these problems urgent.

The Positioning That Drives Everything

"Thirty years building what AI runs on."

Every enterprise right now is trying to figure out AI. Most are buying tools that sit on top of infrastructure they don't understand or trust. eGroup has been building that infrastructure since 1993. They didn't start thinking about these problems when AI became fashionable. They were already there.

This became the hero headline of the new website. Everything else — the design, the navigation, the copy, the calls to action — was built to support and reinforce this single idea.

What The New Site Does NOT Do — And Why

A critical decision was made early: the new site does not try to sell specific products. Not because eGroup doesn't have products, but because the commercial product suite is still being defined and packaged for enterprise sale. Antal's transformation from grant-funded R&D to commercial product company is underway — but not complete.

Trying to lead with half-formed products would undermine the credibility the site is trying to establish. Instead the site does something smarter — it positions eGroup as the company serious enterprises will want to be talking to when they are ready to build real AI infrastructure. It plants the flag. It generates inbound from the right people. Products follow.

The Design Direction

With positioning defined, design direction followed naturally. eGroup needed to look like what it actually is — a world-class deep tech company that happens to be based in Budapest. The reference points were deliberate:

Visual Tone
Dark. Precise.
Not cold — confident. The kind of interface that makes you feel like you're looking at mission-critical infrastructure. Because you are.
Design References
Palantir. Cloudflare.
Companies that communicate technical depth without needing to explain themselves. You look at their sites and just know they're serious.
Primary Typeface
Syne 800
Distinctive, architectural, commands attention at every size. Not Inter, not Space Grotesk — something that gives eGroup an immediately recognisable typographic identity.
Accent Colour
#C8A96E
Warm gold. References depth, quality, and longevity without being flashy. Signals a company with serious history — not a startup chasing hype.

The Homepage — Built in One Session

With positioning and design direction locked, the homepage was built in a single working session. Concept to live URL in hours. No lengthy mockup phase, no committee approval. The page structure follows the positioning logic directly:

Hero — The declaration
"Thirty years building what AI runs on." Full bleed, commanding Syne typography, floating data panel: Est. 1993, Budapest HU, Staff 110+, EU Projects Active, AI Infra Sovereign. Every element reinforces the same idea.
Stats bar — The proof
30+ years. 110+ staff. 5 sectors. EU sovereign partner. Four numbers that say more than four paragraphs of copy ever could.
About — The story
Who eGroup actually is, told plainly and honestly. National infrastructure. EU projects. Medical AI wins. Post-quantum encryption. Antal's quote: "We have been building the infrastructure others will need for decades. The world has finally caught up."
What We Build — The pillars
Three capability pillars: Trust & Identity, Smart Data & AI, Transactional Infrastructure. No specific products yet — deliberately. The pillars are real, proven, and defensible regardless of what the final product suite looks like.
Mission — The belief
"AI without trustworthy infrastructure is just expensive noise. We build the infrastructure." The clearest possible articulation of eGroup's value proposition in one sentence.
Work With Us — The door
Prominent, simple contact form. No friction. Anyone who lands here and thinks "I need to talk to these people" can act on that thought in under 30 seconds.

The Email to Antal

The homepage was not sent to Antal as a bare link with nothing else. An email was written alongside it — explaining the strategic thinking behind every decision — so that his feedback would be informed and useful. The goal: get him reacting to positioning and strategy, not just aesthetics.

The Key Question Put to Antal

"I don't need you to approve the design details — those we can iterate on. What I need to know is whether the positioning feels true. Does 'Intelligence Infrastructure' feel like the right description of where eGroup is heading? Does the emphasis on 30 years of deep tech IP feel accurate and honest? Is there anything fundamental about eGroup's direction that this homepage gets wrong?"

His answer to those questions shapes everything that follows — the full site structure, the product pages as they are defined, and ultimately the tone and content strategy that Rise will automate. We are waiting on that feedback now.
